Good internet safety practices

Objective: By the end of the lesson, students will have a better idea on how to keep themselves safe while exploring the web.

Password Strength

Using a secure password is important because it helps protect your online accounts from being compromised by hackers. A secure password is one that is difficult for someone to guess or crack, making it more difficult for hackers to gain access to your accounts. A strong password typically contains a combination of upper and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols, and is at least 8-12 characters long. Additionally, it's important to use different passwords for each online account you have, so that if one password is compromised, your other accounts are still secure.

Information Sharing

It's important to be careful with information sharing online because the internet is not always a secure and private place. When you share personal information online, such as your name, email address, phone number, or home address, it can be accessed by other people or organizations without your knowledge or consent. This information can be used for identity theft, spamming, phishing attacks, and other types of online scams.

While companies have a responsibility to protect your personal information and keep it secure, there is always a risk that this information can be accessed by unauthorized individuals or organizations. This can happen due to cyberattacks, data breaches, or other security vulnerabilities in the company's systems.

Some best practices are to read the company's privacy policy in which you are giving your information to and limit the amount of personal information you provide.

Email Safety

Be wary of suspicious emails: Be cautious of emails from unknown senders or with suspicious links or attachments. These could be phishing attempts or contain malware that could compromise your account.

Fake emails from legitimate sources: Scammers might send you an email that appears to be from a legitimate source, such as a bank, social media site, or online retailer. The email might ask you to click on a link or enter your login credentials to verify your account, but the link will take you to a fake website designed to steal your information.

Urgent or threatening emails: Scammers might send you an email that claims to be urgent or threatening, such as a notice of a security breach or a message from law enforcement. The email might ask you to take immediate action, such as clicking on a link or sending money to avoid legal consequences.

Fake prize or job offers: Scammers might send you an email that claims you have won a prize or been offered a job. The email might ask you to provide personal information or pay a fee to claim the prize or accept the job offer.

Two Factor Authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is a security process that requires users to provide two different authentication factors to verify their identity before they can access an account or service. The first factor is typically a password, while the second factor can be a fingerprint scan, a voice command, a security token, or a verification code sent to a registered mobile device or email address.

The purpose of 2FA is to provide an extra layer of security that makes it harder for unauthorized users to access your account. Even if an attacker manages to guess or steal your password, they won't be able to access your account without the second factor of authentication.

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is important because it provides an extra layer of security to protect your accounts and personal information. With 2FA, you need to provide two different forms of authentication to access your accounts, such as a password and a verification code sent to your phone.

Software Updates

One of the primary reasons to stay up-to-date with software updates is to ensure that you have the latest security patches. Hackers are always looking for vulnerabilities in software, and companies release updates to fix any security holes that they find. By not updating your software, you may be leaving yourself vulnerable to attacks that could compromise your data or even your entire system.

Updates also often include bug fixes for known issues. These bugs could affect your software's functionality, causing crashes or other errors that can be frustrating to deal with. By updating your software, you can avoid these issues and ensure that everything runs smoothly.

As technology advances, software updates often include changes to ensure that the software remains compatible with new hardware or other software programs. By not updating your software, you may find that it no longer works with other applications or devices that you rely on.

In summary, staying on top of software updates is important to ensure the security, functionality, and compatibility of your software, and to take advantage of new features and improvements.
